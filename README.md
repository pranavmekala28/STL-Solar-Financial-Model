## Overview

A structured investment-grade financial model for an **STL (St. Louis) solar energy project** — built to the standard used in infrastructure and project finance. The model covers the full analytical framework: business assumptions, financial statements, debt schedule, and capital contribution structure.

This is the type of model used by project finance teams at energy companies, infrastructure funds, and investment banks evaluating renewable energy deals.

---

## Model Structure

| Sheet | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `Table of Content` | Full model map — Data Studies overview |
| `Overview` | Project summary, key parameters, and scope |
| `Influence Chart` | Visual map of key business drivers and their interdependencies |
| `Summary Inputs & Assumptions` | Central assumption engine — all key inputs flow from here |
| `Financial Statements` | Projected income statement, balance sheet, and cash flows |
| `Debt` | Debt schedule — drawdowns, repayments, interest, and coverage ratios |
| `Capital Contribution Schedule` | Equity and investor capital call timing |

---

## Key Modeling Components

### Assumptions Engine
All model inputs are centralized in `Summary Inputs & Assumptions` — capacity (MW), capacity factor, power purchase agreement (PPA) pricing, O&M costs, depreciation, and financing structure. Single-point changes cascade through all financial statements.

### Influence Chart
Maps the logical dependency chain from business decisions → financial outputs. Demonstrates which assumptions are most sensitive to project economics (a key tool in investment committee presentations).

### Debt Modeling
- Loan drawdown schedule aligned to construction milestones
- Interest calculations during construction vs. operations phases
- Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) tracking
- Principal repayment schedule

### Capital Contribution Schedule
- Equity investment phasing across project development, construction, and operations
- Investor return modeling foundation
